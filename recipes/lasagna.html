Lasagna

Description

Yields: 6-8

Prep time: 20 minutes

Cook time: 45-60 minutes

A delicious lasagna that will be sure to impress family or guests.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (Italian sausage can also be used)
  • 9-12 no-boil lasagna noodle sheets
  • 1 jar (24-32 ounce) of spaghetti or marinara sauce
  • 1 (15 ounce) container of ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzerella cheese
  • 1 tsp italian seasoning (optional)
  • Salt and peppper to taste
  • Oil or cooking spray

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven & Brown Meat: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease your 9x13 inch baking dish with cooking spray. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium-high heat, breaking it up with a spoon. Drain any excess grease. Stir in the spaghetti/marinara sauce and Italian seasoning (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let it simmer gently for 5 minutes.
  2. Prepare Cheese Mixture: In a large bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, 2 cups mozzarella cheese, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Assemble Lasagna:
    • Spread about 1 cup of the meat sauce on the bottom of your prepared baking dish.
    • Arrange 3-4 no-boil lasagna noodles over the sauce (you might need to break them to fit).
    • Spread about half of the ricotta cheese mixture evenly over the noodles.
    • Top with another layer of meat sauce (about 1.5 cups).
    • Repeat the layers: noodles, remaining ricotta mixture, meat sauce.
    • Place a final layer of noodles on top, then cover with the remaining meat sauce.
    • Sprinkle generously with the remaining mozzarella cheese and a little extra Parmesan cheese.
  4. Bake:
    • Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil.
    • Bake for 30-40 minutes.
    • Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly and lightly golden.
  5. Rest & serve: Let the lasagna rest for at least 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving. This helps it set and prevents it from falling apart.
